Driving in Mongolia as a Foreigner

Foreign travelers may drive in Mongolia if they hold a valid driving license and meet local driving requirements. Travelers from countries that signed the 1968 Vienna Convention on Road Traffic may usually drive with their valid national license and passport.

Some travelers may need an International Driving Permit or additional authorization depending on nationality, vehicle type and travel route.

Road Conditions in Mongolia

  • Mongolia has right-hand traffic.
  • Long distances between towns are common.
  • Some countryside roads are unpaved or off-road.
  • Fuel stations may be limited in remote areas.
  • Mobile signal may be unavailable in some regions.
  • Animals may cross the road without warning.
  • Speed cameras and police checks are common.

Safety Advice

Many road accidents in Mongolia are related to tired driving, drunk driving, speeding, night driving and failure to switch headlights properly.

Never drive when tired or under the influence of alcohol. Avoid long night driving, especially in the countryside.
  • Rest regularly during long-distance driving.
  • Use headlights correctly at night.
  • Follow speed limits.
  • Use offline maps and GPS.
  • Carry enough fuel, water and food for remote routes.
  • Check road and weather conditions before departure.
  • Do not attempt unknown river crossings without guidance.
